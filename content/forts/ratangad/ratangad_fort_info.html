Ratangad Fort

Location: Ahmednagar district of Maharashtra, India

Elevation:   4250 Ft

Mountain range: Sahyadri Mountain Range

Best Time to Visit: October to Dcember

Difficulty Level: Medium-Difficult Depends on Trekking Experience

Base Village: Ratanwadi

Days required for the trek: 1 to 2 days

Type: Hill - Forest - Fort

Description:

Ratangad is a 400 year old fort, captured by Chatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in his time. It is situated in Ratanwadi which is it's base village and overlooks the Bhandardara.

The natural rock formation has an emerging tip at the top known as 'Nedhe' or 'the eye of the needle'. There are many wells on the top each near the four gates of the fort - Ganesh, Hanuman, Konkan, Trimbak.

Ratangad is very popular place for trekking in Maharashtra and also one of the famous Bhandardara tourist places.

How to Reach Ratangad Fort

From Pune:Head towards Shendi Village and then from Shendi you can ask the locals for the route to Ratanwadi which is the base village. The road from Shendi to Ratanwadi is mind blowing. You will get to see untouched nature.

From Mumbai:From Mumbai you can drive to Ratangad Fort through Mumbai-Nashik Highway (NH 3). Head towards Thane – Bhivandi – Asangaon – Igatpuri. Once you reach Ghoti Budruk take right turn and cross the railway tracks. Again take a right turn at Pimpalgaon and one more right to Bari village.

from Igatpuri:To from Igatpuri to Ratangad Fort head towards Ghot Budruk and take a right turn. Head towards Wasali – Baari – Chichondi and reach Ratangad Fort from Shendi. There are many Igatpuri to Bhandardara bus services available and tourist can opt for auto services to reach Ratangad Fort from Igatpuri.
