Top 10 Treks for Beginners in Sahyadri

The western ghat is full of awesome trekking places with different trekking difficulty levels and time to complete the trek. Nowadays, trekking is becoming a passion for many youngsters.

If you are a newcomer or a beginner for the trekking, I would really suggest some good top 10 trekking places which will definitely help you to boost your confidence as well as it will surely increase the love for the Sahyadri mountain range.

Most of the trekking places are near to Mumbai and Pune city.


Here are the top 10 list of trekking locations:

1. Lohagad Fort

Grade Easy
Region Lonavala
Distance from Mumbai 98 Km

 

The Lohagad Fort is one of the most popular forts near Mumbai and Pune. The fort is well preserved and offers an excellent view of the surrounding areas. Must visit spot on fort is the Vinchu kata (scorpionâ€™s tail)

2. Visapur Fort

Grade Easy
Region Lonavala
Distance from Mumbai 98 Km

 

The Visapur Fort is one of the most popular forts near Mumbai and Pune. It is adjacent to Lohgad Fort. It has plenty of water cisterns on the top and a long circular plateau. It is said to have been constructed by Balaji Vishwanath, the first Peshwa of the Maratha Empire and was considered strategically important.

Photos:

3. Sinhgad Fort

Grade Easy
Region Pune
Distance from Pune Junction 32 Km

 

Sinhagad or Sinhagarh means the Lion Fort, is a fortress located southwest of Pune City. Sinhagad Fort is one of the popular places of trekking in Pune and also one of the top places to visit near Pune City. Previously called Kondana, the fort has been the site of many important battles, most notably the Battle of Sinhagad in 1671 CE. The 3 km trek starts from Sinhagarh village is medium and it takes 1 hour to reach the fort. From the top of the fort one can enjoy the majestic view of the mountain valley. The best time to visit the fort is during monsoons.

Photos:

4. Tungareshwar Trek

Grade Easy
Region Vasai

 

Tungareshwar wildlife sanctuary is a high endurance trek near Mumbai (Vasai). The trek is about 18 km long, which can be conveniently divided into 3 parts. The first part of the trek is till the Shiva temple (4 km), the second from Shiva temple till the Balayogi Ashram (7 km) and the final part is the descent from the Ashram to the Vajreshwari road (7 km). Tungareshwar trek is best done in the months of monsoon (June-Septemeber) as there is a stream flowing though the forest area along with series of waterfalls.

Photos:

5. Karnala Fort

Grade Easy
Region Panvel, Raigad District

 

Karnala fort also called Funnel Hill is a hill fort in Raigad District about 10 km from Panvel city, 65km from Mumbai city, situated along the Mumbai-Goa highway. Currently, it is a protected place lying within the Karnala Bird Sanctuary. The fort of Karnala is famous for its strange pinnacle and the bird sanctuary that is set up amidst the forest. Today, the ruins of the fort are a popular destination for hiking and tourism. This Fort is at a height of approx 1500 ft. It is a great trek for beginners and for parents who want to introduce their children to trekking and Mother Nature.

6. Harishchandragad Fort ( Pachnai Village Route )

Grade Easy
Region Igatpuri/Malshej region of Maharashtra

 

Harishchandragad trek via Pachnai is a easy difficulty grade trek in the Sahyadri.

It take around 2 hours to hike this at normal pace. It begins with a small ascent followed by a flat patch and falling into an ascent again.You can stay in the caves near the temple during monsoons. Camping facility available throughout the year. (Villagers provide tents on rent) near the temple as well as on Konkankada.

7. Rajmachi Fort

Grade Easy
Region Igatpuri/Malshej region of Maharashtra

 

Rajmachi Fort has two stronghold forts, Shrivardhan and Manaranjan. Visible from Mumbai Pune Expressway. It is a famous spot for trekking. Rajmachi Fort is 16 km trekking distance from Lonavala there are many routes to reach Rajmachi Fort.

Surrounded by huge waterfalls during monsoon you can view the beautiful Kataldhar Waterfall from Shrivardhan Fort. Rajmachi Fort has many caves used by trekkers for night halt. Pre-monsoon the forest around the forts is glowing with Fireflies at night. You can also trek towards Kondane Caves during monsoon and enjoy the beautiful caves and waterfall.

Photos:

8. Korigad Fort

Grade Easy
Region Lonavala region of Maharashtra

 

Korigad (also called Koraigad, Koarigad or Kumwarigad) is a hill fort located about 20 km south of Lonavla in Pune district, Maharashtra, India. Its date of construction is not known but likely predates 1500. It is about 929 meters above sea level.

There are two lakes on the top of the fort. This fort can be visited at any time of the year. Even a night trek during summer gives a nice experience. There are a lot of camping sites on the fort. There are three temples on the fort which provide ample cover. The Korai Devi temple can accommodate 25 persons.

9. Tikona Fort

Grade Easy
Region Lonavala region of Maharashtra

 

Tikona Fort, literally means Triangle Fort. That's one love triangle we actually want to be in. This trek is special as you can enjoy a splendid view of Pawna Dam and the forts at Visapur, Tung and Lohagad. The fort encloses a lake, Satvahan caves and the temple of â€˜Trimbakeshwar Mahadev.

10. Kothaligad Fort

Grade Medium
Region Karjat region of Maharashtra

 

Kothaligad is more commonly known as the fort of Peth because the village of Peth is situated at its base. The pinnacle of the Peth can be seen from the village. On reaching the top of the fort, we see caves carved in huge rocks.

During monsoon, you can see many beautiful waterfalls below the fort. The fort looks like an inverted funnel, it is also known as funnel hill. Kothaligad Fort is famous for its night trek during winter and summer months. During fireflies season one can also spot fireflies near the fort.
